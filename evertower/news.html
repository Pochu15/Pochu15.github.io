Development updates

Hello! I'm Carlos and you're reading the third development update of Evertower Lite. As I said in last post, this month has been way less intense than last one. Sadly, I couldn't implement all the things I wanted to yet, but I still made much progress. So, let's get started!

Infinite gameplay

Now, dungeons will generate a door to the next level! Crossing this door will trigger dungeon generation again, refreshing the dungeon. It'll also increase the difficulty of the dungeon, increasing some stats of the monsters that you'll have to fight. It also increases their loot.

Each monster is affected by difficulty in a different way. For example, Blob will gain resistance and extra HP while Bat swarms will gain extra damage and extra healing multiplier. This makes each enemy feel more unique.

Hold on!

As you can see in the screenshot above, there's now a pause button, which lets you pause the game! In this pause menu, you'll be able to exit the run at any time. And you'll also be able to edit your starting deck if you're not currently on a run.

Did you say deck edit?

Yes! After half a year of development, you can finally edit your starting deck. To edit it, you'll have to Unlock a new permanent upgrade, which will allow you to enter the deck editing panel. Once in this panel, you'll be able to create a starting deck with the cards that you've already unlocked.

To make it fair (...to prevent the player from creating an overpowered deck), you'll have a limit of 136 magic dust, meaning that you can add as many cards as you want, as long as you don't surpass the dust limit. And if 136 dust doesn't seem enough, well, don't worry! With the help of the new permanent upgrades, you'll be able to increase this number up to 776!

New content!

New monsters

I've added two new enemies to the game!

  • Cave spider: Every game needs a spider, right? This little fella has two attacks:
    • Spider bomb: Deal 20 damage
    • Bite: Deal 20 damage and Poison the target, dealing an extra 10 damage over 5 turns.
    This monster can deal a lot of damage with its poison, as it can stack multiple times. Poison lasts only one battle.
    This monster has 80 HP, and gains 30 extra HP and 20% extra damage with each level.
  • Extinguish-bot: A fire extinguisher is attacking you? Well, you must be lit!
    • Extinguish: Deal 25 damage
    • Shield nova: Grant 20 shield points to all your team
    You might want to prioritize this monster, as you don't want it shielding all of its allies. It also deals a lot of damage with its attack!
    This monster only has 40 HP. It gains 20 extra HP, 10% extra damage and the power of Shield nova increases by 20 with each level.

New permanent upgrades

As mentioned before, you can now unlock deck editing and gain more dust to your starting deck with new permanent upgrades.

You can also increase your starting hand size and your maximum hand size, from (2-3) to (5-7), with two new permanent upgrades.

New spells

I didn't want to include this as first, as I had planned to add eleven new spells, but I only had time to add three. Although I'm still working on this, here's what I've been able to implement so far!

  • Meditate: (Arcane, 35 Mana) Remove up to three negative effects (like poison), and gain 10% of your total HP as a shield.
  • Infinite knowledge: (Arcane, 20 Mana, Fast) Draw three cards.
  • Tornado: (Tempest, 40 Mana, Fast) Deal 80 damage to an enemy. If it dies, recover 10 Mana and return this to your hand.

Minor changes

  • Discharge won't heal anymore; it'll instead grant you a shield based on the damage you did. (Tempest, 20 Mana, Fast) Deal 20 damage, gain all the damage you did as a shield.
  • Resistance has been slightly nerfed again.
  • Bat swarm's spells had their damage increased.
  • Tempest boomerang's damage has been adjusted, from 40/50/75 to 50/60/75

Hello, and thanks for reading the second devolepment post of Evertower Lite! July has been a busy month in which I added a lot of content. So, without further ado, let's review all the changes I've done!

Shops

Now the dungeons also generate shops! Here, you will be able to spend gold by purchasing items. You will also be able to purchase Legendary items, a new addition to the game which I'll talk about later.

Zahara, the vendor, will offer the player three random items, and a legendary one.

Legendary Items

During this month, I wanted to add more content to the game. The biggest addition is, with no doubt, legendary items.

Legendary items are very powerful items which will shape your run. They provide a huge advantage, but sometimes they'll force you to change your deck a bit to make the most out of them. This encourages the player to try new strategies each run, thus making each run feel unique. Here's a list of the items I've created!

  • Uurmi's urn. This item will summon a companion to help you. Uurmi will cast a random spell from its pool at the beggining of your turn. This item is really good, but it's not very reliable, as the action Uurmi takes is random.
    • Right now, Uurmi can give you a shield, deal damage to a random target or recover a small ammount of mana. However, I'm planning to add more spells to its pool to make it more random.
  • Giant scepter. This item will give you a huge damage boost, but will make your spells cost twice as much.
  • Ancient tome. This item will transform all the mana you spend in arcane spells into a shield. This effect obviously encourages the player to build its deck around arcane spells.
  • Neverending glass. This glass will recover health and mana at the end of your turn, but only if you're shielded.
  • The grave digger. This shovel will heal you a small ammount of health whenever an enemy dies.
  • Hero's lost sword. This sword will give you a 10% damage boost at the start of each turn while battling. This item becomes really useful on long battles.

The only way to obtain legendary items now is by purchasing them on a shop. However, you don't get to pick what item you buy; you'll get one random instead. Additionaly, each legendary item in your backpack increases the cost of future legendary items.

Even more content

I've added a ton of spells this month, and I've also added a new element, fire, which focuses on dealing damage over time.

    New arcane spells

  • Seeker's trap, a fast spell (doesn't end your turn when you use it) which decreases an enemy defense by 40% until the start of its turn.
  • Magic bubble, a spell which will give you 60 Shield points, and will increase your defense by 20% while you're shielded.

    • New tempest spells

  • Bifurcated lightning, a powefull spell that deals 70 damage to an enemy, and any excess damage is dealt to all other enemies.
  • Sparkling armour, a cheap spell that will zap your enemies when they attack you, dealing them 20 damage.

    • New fire spells

  • Fireball, a cheap spell (15 mana) that deals 30 damage to a target, and another 30 over 3 turns.
  • Inferno, a really expensive spell which deals 40 damage to a target and then 40 damage to all targets. It also adds a 60 damage burn (20 damage each turn during 3 turns) to all enemies.

Of course, all these spells have to be unlocked from the bookstand. This brings the total of spells currently in the game to eleven, and I'm panning to add more on each update

Minor changes

I added a shield system. Being shielded temporarily increases your health points. Shield is lost at the end of each battle.

I reworked the armour system. It used to have a cap (70% max armour) to prevent the player from achieving a 100% resistance, which would break the game. Howevewer, this would make defensive items useless, as they will be pointless once you reached the maximum resistance value. The solution was to make armour items less impactful the more armour you have. For example, a Hogo stone with 0% resistance will increase your resistance to 10%, while the same item with 50% resistance will only increase it to 55%.

I added new permanent upgrades, this time, to boost your damage. Here's some art!

Testing, testing... it works! Welcome to the first Evertower Lite Development post! In these posts, I'll explain the changes I made to the game as well as the features I plan to develop.

What I've done

So far, Evertower Lite has:

  • A permanent upgrades system, which allows you to gain permanent buffs like extra health or unlock new spells.
  • An inventory system, with support for both passive and active items.
  • A spell system. Each character has a deck where spells are held. At the beggining of each battle, that deck is shuffled. Players can only use spells from their hands (and will draw spells at the beggining of each turn), and these spells are shuffled back in the deck after finishing the battle.
    • Spells, items and permanent upgrades work with a list of effects. Each spell has a list of effects that are activated when the spell is casted.
  • A random dungeon generation system, built so that it's easy to add dungeon elements to the generation pool. Each dungeon currently has:
    • Chests, which will give the player loot.
    • Enemies, which will trigger battle mode.
    • Doors, which will take you from one point in the dungeon to another.
    • Crafting tables, which will allow you to craft unlocked recipes.
  • A fully functioning Battle system.
    • Although no items in the game currently implement this, each battling character has an event system. As for now, there's only six battle-events: On battle start, On turn start, On turn end, After taking damage, Before casting a spell and after casting a spell. When any of this events is triggered, it'll activate all of the effects associated to it. This opens the door to very interesting features like poison damage, which would cause damage at the end of each turn, or getting healed up after dealing damage. The possibilities this enables are endless.
  • Data persistancy. Although there's still room for improvement, player data is stored. Concretely, a permanent upgrades data file will be created, and will be loaded at the beggining of the game. If the player quits while doing a dungeon, data will also be stored. There's no save button yet, but data is stored when you end a battle, when you open a chest or when you craft something. Dying will erase your dungeon data.
